## How to Use It (4 Easy Steps)

### Step 1: Read the Medical Disclaimer ‚ö†Ô∏è

The red disclaimer section at the top explains:
- This calculator is for **reference only**
- Always follow your **doctor's orders**
- Individual needs may vary

**This is important!** Make sure to understand that this tool helps you understand the math, but your medical team's specific instructions always come first.

### Step 2: Enter Patient Information

Fill in three simple fields:

1. **Weight:** Patient's weight (automatic conversion between units!)
   - Enter in **Kilograms (kg)**: Example: 70 kg
   - OR enter in **Pounds (lbs)**: Example: 154 lbs
   - üí° **Tip:** If you enter the weight in one unit, it automatically converts to the other!

2. **Height:** Patient's height (automatic conversion between units!)
   - Enter in **Centimeters (cm)**: Example: 170 cm
   - OR enter in **Inches (in)**: Example: 67 inches
   - üí° **Tip:** If you enter the height in one unit, it automatically converts to the other!
   - *OR* skip this and enter BMI if you already have it

3. **Formula Type:** Choose from dropdown
   - Standard Formula (1.0 cal/mL) - most common
   - Peptamen AF (1.2 cal/mL) - high calorie
   - Impact Peptide 1.5 (1.5 cal/mL) - highest calorie

### Step 3: Click "Calculate"

The app will:
- Calculate BMI
- Determine daily caloric needs
- Figure out how much volume is needed per day
- Calculate the goal feeding rate (mL/hour)

### Step 4: Review the Results

You'll see:

#### Key Results Card (Green)
- **Daily Caloric Needs:** How many calories patient needs per day
- **Daily Volume:** Total mL to give over 24 hours
- **Goal Feeding Rate:** Target mL per hour
- **Time to Goal:** Hours to reach full speed with standard advancement

#### Feeding Protocol (Purple)
Shows the step-by-step advancement:
- Start at 20 mL/hr
- Increase by 20 mL every 4 hours
- Until you reach the goal rate

Example: If goal is 90 mL/hr:
- Hour 0: Start 20 mL/hr
- Hour 4: Advance to 40 mL/hr
- Hour 8: Advance to 60 mL/hr
- Hour 12: Advance to 80 mL/hr
- Hour 16: Advance to 90 mL/hr (goal reached!)
- Every 4 hours: 30 mL water flush

## Common Questions

**Q: The calculation seems different from what the doctor prescribed?**  
A: This calculator uses general guidelines. Your doctor may have specific orders based on your patient's individual situation. Always follow the doctor's prescribed rate.

**Q: Can I print this?**  
A: Your browser's print function should work (Ctrl+P or Cmd+P). The layout is mobile-friendly so it should print nicely.

**Q: What if the patient is overweight?**  
A: The calculator automatically uses a special formula for overweight patients to avoid over-feeding. It calculates ideal body weight + 10%.

**Q: Can I use this for a child?**  
A: Not recommended. Children have different caloric needs. Check with your pediatric team for appropriate calculations.

**Q: The results seem high/low?**  
A: Check these:
- Weight units are correct (kilograms, not pounds)
- Height is in centimeters
- Formula is correctly selected
- No typos in the numbers

**Q: Can I calculate multiple patients?**  
A: Yes! Just click "Reset" and enter new information for each patient.
